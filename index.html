💡 Korea Money Pro Tips
🙅
No tipping culture! Unlike the US or Europe, tipping is not expected — and can even be considered rude in Korea. Remove tips from your budget entirely.
🚇
T-money Card saves 30%+ on transit. Buy a T-money card at any convenience store for ₩2,500 and enjoy discounted fares + free transfers within 30 minutes.
🏪
Convenience stores are your best friend. CU, GS25, 7-Eleven — you can get a full meal (triangle kimbap + ramen) for under ₩3,000. Open 24/7 everywhere.
💳
Cash vs Card: Most places accept cards, but traditional markets (시장) and street food stalls are cash only. Keep ₩50,000 cash handy.
🍜
Eat where locals eat. "백반집" (Baekban restaurants) offer a full meal with rice + 4-5 side dishes for just ₩7,000~9,000. Way cheaper than tourist areas.
